Sunday Night Baseball Pool

This is the Sunday night baseball squares pool.
27 ESPN Sunday night games and the All star game.

The Pool

Format

There will be a 7x7 square system in place for a total of 49 squares. The numbers being used will be 0-6. The home team will ALWAYS be on top and the visiting team will ALWAYS be on the side. The numbers 7, 8 and 9 are out of play. Square numbers will be redrawn each week.

Games

There will be a total of 28 games for the season. The games will be the weekly ESPN Sunday Night Baseball game and the All Star game.

Rules

  1. The score after 9 innings will be the score that determines that week’s winner, even if that score is a tie. The reason for this makes matching numbers beneficial.
  2. If the final score, after 9 innings, ends in a 7, 8 or 9 there will be NO winner that week. Instead the winnings will be pushed until the next week.
  3. If the final score ends on an unfilled square that week’s winnings will be pushed to the next week’s game.
  4. If a game gets rained out then the replacement game will be ESPN Monday Night Baseball the following night.
  5. If a game is started and gets rained out before it is an official game then the replacement game will be the following nights ESPN Monday Night Baseball game.
  6. If a game gets rained out after it is an official game then the final score will determine the winner.
  7. If there is no winner for the last game of the year the deciding game for the remaining winnings will be the World Series, Game 1. If there is no winner during Game 1 each following World Series game will be the deciding game until there is a winner.
